## 📌 Project

### Business Intelligence Analytics for U.S. E-Commerce Performance Optimization
**A Multi-Dimensional Analysis of Sales, Customer Behaviour, and Delivery Operations (2019–2025)**

The U.S. e-commerce industry exceeded 1.1 trillion USD in online sales by 2024. This project builds a Business Intelligence solution to help e-commerce businesses turn large volumes of transactional data into actionable insights — covering revenue performance, customer behaviour, payment preferences, and delivery operations.

---

## 🛠️ Methodology & Tools

The project followed a full BI workflow:

1. **Data Integration** — Combined multi-source datasets covering products, payments, customer satisfaction, and delivery performance
2. **Data Cleaning & Transformation** — Removed duplicates, corrected data type inconsistencies, generated calculated fields (profit margin, discount rate, delivery delay)
3. **Dashboard Development** — Built interactive Power BI dashboards with slicers, KPI cards, and storyboards

**Tools Used:** Power BI · Excel · ETL pipeline

---

## 📈 Dashboards & Key Insights

### Dashboard: E-Commerce Customer Journey Storyboard
A storyboard combining all main visualisations — connecting revenue trends, payment behaviour, and delivery operations into a single business narrative.

### Key Insights

| Area | Finding |
|------|---------|
| **Top Revenue Category** | Electronics dominates at ~$732.78M, followed by Furniture ($109.09M) and Fashion ($67.82M) |
| **Revenue Trend** | Steady growth from 2019–2025 with observable seasonal fluctuations |
| **Delivery Performance** | Delivery delay rate varies significantly by state — high-activity regions show higher delay rates |
| **Payment Method** | Donut chart reveals dominant payment preferences among customers |

### Visualisation Types Used
- **Line Charts** — Revenue trends over time (2019–2025)
- **Bar Charts** — Revenue by product category, order volume by state
- **Donut Charts** — Payment method distribution
- **KPI Cards & Tables** — Total revenue, total orders, average review scores
- **Slicers** — Dynamic filtering by year

## 💭 Reflection

This project gave me hands-on experience turning raw e-commerce transaction data into a real decision-support tool. The data cleaning phase was more involved than expected — duplicates, type mismatches, and missing calculated fields all had to be resolved before any meaningful visualisation could happen. Building the Power BI dashboards taught me how to choose the right chart type for each question: line charts for trends, bar charts for comparisons, donut charts for proportions. Seeing all the insights come together in the Customer Journey storyboard — where revenue, payments, and delivery are connected — made me understand why BI is valued in business: it turns scattered data into a story that decision-makers can actually act on.
